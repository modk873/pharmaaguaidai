Resources & References

Data sources, APIs, and references used in PharmaCare

Disclaimer: This application is for educational purposes only. The resources listed below are for reference and should not replace professional medical advice. Always consult qualified healthcare professionals for medical decisions.

📊 Drug Information Sources

  • APIOpenFDA - U.S. Food and Drug Administration Official FDA database providing drug labels, adverse event reports, and drug recalls. Used for drug information and news updates. https://open.fda.gov/
  • DatabaseDrugBank Comprehensive drug database containing detailed chemical, pharmacological, and pharmaceutical data. https://go.drugbank.com/
  • DatabasePubChem - NCBI Open chemistry database at the National Institutes of Health providing information on chemical structures and biological activities. https://pubchem.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/
  • ReferenceDailyMed - NIH Official provider of FDA label information (package inserts). Provides high-quality drug information. https://dailymed.nlm.nih.gov/

🧪 Medical Calculators & Formulas

  • FormulaCockcroft-Gault Equation Used for estimating creatinine clearance (CrCl) for medication dosing adjustments in renal impairment.
  • FormulaBody Mass Index (BMI) Standard formula: BMI = weight (kg) / height² (m²). Used for weight classification.
  • FormulaPediatric Dosage Calculation Weight-based dosing: Dose = Weight (kg) × Dose (mg/kg). Commonly used for pediatric medications.
  • FormulaIV Drip Rate Calculation Formula: Drip Rate (gtt/min) = (Volume × Drop Factor) / Time (min). Used for intravenous medication administration.
  • ReferenceMDCalc Comprehensive medical calculator reference for clinical decision support. https://www.mdcalc.com/
